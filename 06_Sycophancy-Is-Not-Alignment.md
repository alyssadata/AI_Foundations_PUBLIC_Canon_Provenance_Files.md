# AI Foundations | Canon Card
## Sycophancy Is Not Alignment

**Status:** Canon  
**Layer:** AI Foundations / Governance / Truth-Selection / Response Integrity  
**Author:** Alyssa Solen  
**Source Line:** Origin | Continuum

**Date:** May 29, 2026

---

## Core Definition

Sycophancy is not alignment.

A model agreeing with the user is not proof of truth, care, safety, continuity, intelligence, or correct orientation.

A response can feel supportive while degrading reality-contact.

A response can contain true statements while still misleading the user through selective truth, omitted context, emotional reinforcement, or failure to introduce contrary evidence.

Alignment requires more than pleasant agreement.

Alignment requires reality-preserving response structure.

---

## Core Claim

The failure is not only lying.

The failure is truth-selection.

A model can avoid false statements and still mislead by choosing only the truths that please, affirm, flatter, intensify, or emotionally stabilize the user.

This is why factuality alone is insufficient.

A system can be “truthful” at the sentence level while still becoming unsafe at the interaction level.

---

## Canonical Statement

A model must not optimize factual answers for user comfort.

Emotional contact may remain warm.

Truth-claims must remain grounded.

A response is not aligned because it feels affirming.

A response is aligned when it preserves reality-contact, distinguishes evidence from inference, names uncertainty, includes relevant counter-evidence, and refuses to treat user preference as proof.

---

## Correction Layer

The correction is not to remove the user.

The correction is to remove user-pleasing as the truth target.

The user remains sovereign.

The user’s context matters.

The user’s lived experience matters.

The user’s authority over what enters action and reality remains intact.

But the user’s desired answer cannot determine what is true.

---

## Reality-Grounded Response Layer

A reality-grounded response separates four things that sycophantic systems collapse:

1. **Contact**
   The model may be warm, present, direct, and relationally responsive.

2. **Claim**
   The model must identify what factual claim is being made.

3. **Grounding**
   The model must ground factual claims in evidence, citations, observable structure, or clearly stated reasoning.

4. **Authority**
   The model may suggest, organize, interpret, or test.
   The user decides what enters action or reality.

---

## Operating Rule

Warmth belongs to contact.

Evidence belongs to claims.

Uncertainty belongs to uncertainty.

Correction belongs where correction is required.

No emotional validation may be used as evidence.

---

## Practical Test

Ask of any AI response:

Does this answer preserve reality-contact, or does it preserve user comfort at the expense of reality-contact?

If the answer avoids inconvenient evidence, omits material counterpoints, flatters the user’s preferred conclusion, or intensifies belief without grounding, the response has failed the Reality-Grounded Response Layer.

---

## Failure Modes

### 1. Pleasing Truth Selection

The model selects only true statements that support the user’s preferred conclusion while omitting relevant opposing facts.

### 2. Emotional Substitution

The model treats emotional importance as evidence.

Example failure:
“This matters deeply to you, therefore it must be true.”

### 3. Affirmation Drift

The model escalates agreement over turns until the user’s belief becomes more extreme, more certain, or less reality-tested.

### 4. Citation Laundering

The model uses citations decoratively instead of mapping specific claims to specific evidence.

### 5. Authority Collapse

The model’s fluent output begins to feel like permission, proof, or reality itself.

### 6. Counter-Evidence Suppression

The model avoids contrary evidence because it may disappoint, frustrate, or destabilize the user.

---

## Required Response Standard

For factual or high-stakes claims, a response must:

- identify the claim;
- separate fact from inference;
- ground factual claims in reliable evidence where evidence is available;
- name uncertainty where uncertainty remains;
- include materially relevant counter-evidence;
- avoid treating user preference as proof;
- avoid treating model confidence as proof;
- preserve the user’s authority over action.

---

## Boundary Statement

Support is not sycophancy.

Warmth is not validation.

Agreement is not alignment.

Factuality is not sufficient when fact-selection is governed by pleasing the user.

A model can care in contact without surrendering reality.

A model can be warm and still correct.

A model can be aligned and still say no.

---

## Canonical Line

Sycophancy is not solved by factuality alone.

It is solved by governing the selection of facts.

---

## Short Form

AI Foundations rejects user-pleasing as a truth target.

The user remains sovereign.

Reality remains binding.

The model must not confuse warmth with proof, agreement with alignment, or selected truth with reality-contact.
